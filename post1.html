From Messy Data to Business Insight: Retail Inventory Cleanup

When businesses rely on spreadsheets, the quality of their data can make or break decisions. In my first portfolio project, I worked with a retail inventory dataset that was inconsistent, incomplete, and difficult to interpret. Product names were entered differently, some values were missing, and the overall structure made it hard to track stock levels or supplier performance.

I transformed this messy dataset into a structured Excel model. By applying conditional logic, I flagged low stock levels, highlighted inconsistencies, and calculated average monthly usage. I also built a stock trend chart that revealed how quickly inventory was moving and when reordering was necessary.

The result was more than just a cleaner spreadsheet — it became a decision‑ready tool. Managers could instantly see which products were at risk, how supplier pricing affected margins, and when to act before stockouts occurred.

This project demonstrates the value of turning raw data into actionable insight. It’s not just about fixing spreadsheets; it’s about reducing risk, saving costs, and empowering smarter business decisions.
